Rotation and Scaling Transformation of a Parallelogram

Rotation is a fundamental geometric transformation that involves changing the orientation of an object around a specified point (the center of rotation). Clockwise rotation refers to the rotation in the direction of a clock's hands, i.e., a decrease in the angle between a reference axis and the object. In 2D space, a clockwise rotation of a point (x, y) by an angle θ can be achieved using the following equations: x' = x * cos(θ) - y * sin(θ) y' = x * sin(θ) + y * cos(θ) Where (x', y') are the new coordinates of the point after rotation Scaling involves changing the size of an object by multiplying its coordinates by a scale factor. Scaling can be uniform (scaling by the same factor in both dimensions) or non-uniform (scaling by different factors in each dimension). In the case of uniform scaling by a factor A, the new coordinates (x', y') of a point (x, y) are given by: x' = A * x y' = A * y To perform a rotation and scaling simultaneously, you can apply the scaling operation after the rotation. For a clockwise rotation followed by scaling by a factor A, you can apply the rotation transformation first and then multiply the coordinates by A.

Transformation of a Parallelogram

A parallelogram is a quadrilateral with opposite sides that are parallel. To transform a parallelogram based on a selected vertex, you can translate the parallelogram so that the selected vertex coincides with the origin. Apply the rotation and scaling transformations to each vertex of the parallelogram relative to the origin. After transformation, translate the parallelogram back to its original position by adding the coordinates of the selected vertex.
